First Aid & Emergency Procedures

Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)

Recognize these signs and act immediately:

  • Difficulty breathing or wheezing
  • Swelling of the tongue or throat
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Rapid pulse
  • Hives over large areas of the body
Call Emergency Services

How to Use an Epinephrine Auto-Injector

[Epinephrine Injection Tutorial Video]

1

Remove the safety cap

Grip the auto-injector firmly and pull off the blue safety release with your other hand.

2

Place against outer thigh

Press the orange tip against the outer thigh at a 90-degree angle (can be done through clothing).

3

Administer the injection

Push down firmly until you hear a "click" and hold for 3 seconds to deliver the medication.

4

Seek immediate medical help

Call emergency services even if symptoms improve. The effects may wear off and symptoms may return.
